My Journey into Computer Engineering

My name is Akyerem Denny-Kelly, born in the year 2008 to Mr. and Mrs. Atsrim. I am the fourth of five children in my family. I hail from Kpedze in the Volta Region of Ghana, though I currently reside in Ho, the regional capital. Being a Christian by faith, I uphold values of discipline and hard work. I had my early education at Kabore School Complex, a military-governed institution in the Volta Region. After completing my basic education, I proceeded to Mawuli School for my secondary education, where I studied General Science after passing the Basic Education Certificate Examination (BECE) in Junior High School 1. Presently, I am pursuing a degree in Computer Engineering at Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST), where I aim to deepen my knowledge in both hardware and software development.

Why Computer Engineering?

From a young age, I have been fascinated by technology and gadgets. I was always eager to open up electronic devices, explore their internal components, and attempt to fix them when they malfunctioned. Even though my early attempts at repairing gadgets were not always successful, my determination never wavered. I vividly remember the first time I successfully repaired my gamepad—it was a small victory, but it sparked a lifelong passion for technology. At that time, my knowledge of computers, coding, and engineering concepts was very limited. Initially, I had an interest in medicine, but as I grew older, I found myself drawn more toward computers and machines. I became intrigued by how they functioned, how software interacted with hardware, and how technology could be leveraged to solve real-world problems. By the time I reached senior high school, I had made up my mind—I wanted to pursue Computer Engineering. I realized that this field would offer me the opportunity to expand my understanding of both computer hardware and software, enabling me to build innovative solutions and contribute to technological advancements. Now, as a Computer Engineering student, I am eager to learn everything I can about this dynamic field. My goal is to develop skills in programming, hardware design, and system optimization, ultimately becoming a proficient engineer who can make meaningful contributions to the tech industry.
